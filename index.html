What is a circadian wheel?

Your internal clock runs on a cycle called the tau (Greek τ). For most people it's very close to 24 hours — daylight keeps it synchronized, so you wake up at roughly the same time each morning.

For some people, the clock runs longer: 24.5h, 25h, or more. Without external anchors, sleep drifts progressively later each day, cycling through all 24 hours of the clock roughly every two to six weeks. This is Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Disorder.

The wheel shows where you are in your personal cycle right now. The outer ring maps your cycle against the 24-hour clock. The colors reflect the actual sky at each hour — dawn, daylight, dusk, night.

Rikki Delaine, who built this tool, has a measured tau of 25.40 hours — 84 minutes of drift per day. Their personal real-time tracking page: rikkikkir.github.io/circadian
